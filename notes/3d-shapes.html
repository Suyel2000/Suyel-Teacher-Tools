3D SHAPES

Solid shapes that have length, width and height.

They have thickness and occupy space.

Quick Facts

  • 3D shapes are solid.
  • They have length, width and height.
  • They occupy space.
  • They have faces, edges and vertices (corners).
  • Not all 3D shapes roll.
  • Some can slide, some can roll.
PARTS OF A 3D SHAPE
Face — Flat surface of a shape
Edge — Line where two faces meet
Vertex (Corner) — Point where edges meet
TYPES OF 3D SHAPES
1. CUBE
  • Faces: 6 (all square)
  • Edges: 12
  • Vertices: 8
  • All faces are equal squares.
Examples:
Rubik's cube
Dice
2. CUBOID
  • Faces: 6 (all rectangular)
  • Edges: 12
  • Vertices: 8
  • Opposite faces are equal.
Examples:
Book
Shoe box
3. SPHERE
  • Faces: 1 (curved)
  • Edges: 0
  • Vertices: 0
  • Rolls in all directions.
Examples:
Ball
Orange
4. CYLINDER
  • Faces: 3 (2 flat + 1 curved)
  • Edges: 2 (curved)
  • Vertices: 0
  • Rolls on its curved surface.
Examples:
Tin can
Pipe
5. CONE
  • Faces: 2 (1 flat + 1 curved)
  • Edges: 1 (curved)
  • Vertices: 1
  • Rolls on its curved surface.
Examples:
Ice cream cone
Party hat
6. SQUARE PYRAMID
  • Faces: 5 (4 triangular + 1 square)
  • Edges: 8
  • Vertices: 5
  • Has a square base.
Examples:
Egyptian pyramid
Tent
7. TRIANGULAR PYRAMID
  • Faces: 4 (all triangular)
  • Edges: 6
  • Vertices: 4
  • Has a triangular base.
Examples:
Toblerone chocolate
Pyramid model
8. RECTANGULAR PYRAMID
  • Faces: 5 (4 triangular + 1 rectangular)
  • Edges: 8
  • Vertices: 5
  • Has a rectangular base.
Examples:
Monument
Roof top
9. TRIANGULAR PRISM
  • Faces: 5 (2 triangular + 3 rectangular)
  • Edges: 9
  • Vertices: 6
  • Two triangular bases.
Examples:
Toblerone pack
Roof truss
10. RECTANGULAR PRISM
  • Faces: 6 (all rectangular)
  • Edges: 12
  • Vertices: 8
  • Opposite faces are equal.
Examples:
Brick
Eraser
