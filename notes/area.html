🧩 Section 1: What is Area?

Imagine you want to cover your classroom floor with colorful square tiles. The total amount of space covered by those tiles is called the AREA!

DEFINING AREA

"The amount of space inside a closed shape."

🔍

Inside Part

Area measures the flat space filled inside a boundary.

🔲

Square Units

We count how many standard square tiles fit inside.

📏

Not Perimeter

Perimeter is the outer border. Area is the inside surface!

📊 Section 3: Understanding Area with Grids

To easily measure area, we divide shapes into Rows (left to right) and Columns (top to bottom) of equal square tiles!

Area simply means counting ALL the square tiles packed inside!

🔢 Section 5: How to Find Area (Step-by-Step)

You don't always have to count every single square one by one! Follow these 3 easy steps:

STEP 1 📏

Measure Length

Find out how long the shape is along the top or bottom.

STEP 2 📐

Measure Breadth

Find out how wide or tall the shape is along the side.

STEP 3 ✖️

Multiply Them!

Multiply Length × Breadth to get total Area instantly!

✨ Area = Length × Breadth ✨

📐 Section 6: Area Formulas

▭ Rectangle Formula

A rectangle has 2 longer sides (Length) and 2 shorter sides (Breadth).

Area = Length × Breadth

A = L × B

⏹️ Square Formula

A square has 4 equal sides! So length and breadth are identical.

Area = Side × Side

A = Side²

📌 Important Golden Rule: Always write your final answer in Square Units (such as cm² or m²)!

🤔 Section 12: Competency-Based Thinking Questions

Q1: Can two shapes have the EXACT SAME Perimeter but DIFFERENT Areas?

Answer: YES! Consider Shape A (Rectangle 5cm × 1cm: Perimeter = 12cm, Area = 5cm²) and Shape B (Rectangle 3cm × 3cm: Perimeter = 12cm, Area = 9cm²). Same perimeter, totally different areas!

Q2: What happens to the area of a square if you DOUBLE the length of its side?

Answer: The Area becomes 4 TIMES bigger! For example, a 2cm square has area 4cm². If side becomes 4cm, area becomes 16cm²!

Q3: Why can't area be measured in centimeters (cm) instead of square centimeters (cm²)?

Answer: Centimeters only measure length in a straight line (1D). Area measures a 2-dimensional surface, so we need 2D square tiles (cm²)!

Q4: If a rectangle is split diagonally into 2 equal triangles, what is the area of each triangle?

Answer: Exactly HALF the area of the rectangle!

Q5: If you join two squares of side 4 cm side-by-side, what is the area of the new shape?

Answer: 32 cm². (Each square has area 16 cm², so 16 + 16 = 32 cm²).

📖 Section 13: Solved Real-Life Word Problems

Sol: Area = 8 × 6 = 48 m²

Sol: Area = 5 × 5 = 25 m²

Sol: Area = 12 × 5 = 60 m²

⚠️ Section 14: Common Mistakes to Avoid!

❌ Mistake 1: Adding instead of Multiplying

Wrong: Area = 6 + 4 = 10 cm

✔️ Correct: Area = 6 × 4 = 24 cm²

❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting "Square" Units

Wrong: Area = 24 cm

✔️ Correct: Area = 24 cm² (or sq cm)

❌ Mistake 3: Measuring Boundary Lines

Counting the outer line length measures Perimeter, NOT Area!

✔️ Correct: Count inside tiles for Area!

❌ Mistake 4: Mixing Units (cm & m)

Multiplying 2 m by 50 cm directly without converting first!

✔️ Correct: Always convert both measurements to the same unit first!

📖 Section 22: Primary Class Glossary

Area:
The total amount of flat space contained inside a closed 2D shape.
Square Unit:
A standard square measuring 1 unit on all sides, used as a building block to count total area.
Dimension:
A measurable distance along one direction, such as Length or Breadth.
