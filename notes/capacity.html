MEASURING CAPACITY

Capacity tells us how much a container can hold.
We measure capacity using standard units! 🧪
WHAT IS CAPACITY?
  • Capacity is the amount of liquid a container can hold.
  • It is different from weight.
  • We measure capacity using standard units like millilitres and litres.
A bigger container has more capacity. A smaller container has less capacity!
UNITS OF CAPACITY
Millilitre
(mL)
Used to measure small amounts of liquid (e.g., spoon, small glass)
Litre
(L)
Used to measure larger amounts of liquid (e.g., water bottle, bucket)
🌟 1 Litre (L) = 1000 Millilitres (mL) 🌟
QUICK FACTS
  • We measure capacity using litre (L) and millilitre (mL).
  • 1 litre (L) = 1000 millilitres (mL)
  • We use the right tool for accurate measurement.
  • Capacity depends on the size of the container.
  • Containers can have the same capacity even if their shapes are different.
  • Read the liquid level at the bottom of the meniscus (curve).
Always read from the bottom of the meniscus curve!

CONVERSION EXAMPLES
1. Convert L to mL: 2 L = 2 × 1000 = mL
2. Half Litre value: ½ L = 1000 ÷ 2 = mL
3. Quarter Litre value: ¼ L = 1000 ÷ 4 = mL
4. Complex Multiplier Check: 3 L = 3 × 1000 = mL
