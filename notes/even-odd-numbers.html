EVEN AND ODD NUMBERS

Numbers can be even or odd. Let's learn how to find them!
WHAT ARE THEY?

EVEN NUMBERS end in
0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.

They can be divided into 2 equal groups with no remainder.

ODD NUMBERS end in
1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.

They cannot be divided into 2 equal groups.

Look at the ones digit to find out!

EVEN NUMBERS
0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, ...
Examples:

These 6 apples can be divided into 2 equal groups.

ODD NUMBERS
1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15, ...
Examples:

These 5 apples cannot be divided into 2 equal groups.

QUICK FACTS
  • 0 is an even number.
  • All even numbers are multiples of 2.
  • All odd numbers are not multiples of 2.
  • Even numbers always come in pairs.
  • Odd numbers come one by one.

Finding absolute rules helps map patterns fast!

IN REAL LIFE

We see even and odd numbers everywhere!

Shoes come in pairs.
2, 4, 6, ... even numbers!

If one egg is extra, the total is odd.

The hours on a clock:
Even - 2,4,6,8,10,12
Odd - 1,3,5,7,9,11

If friends stand in pairs, leftover one means odd!

KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER
  • Even numbers end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
  • Odd numbers end in 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
