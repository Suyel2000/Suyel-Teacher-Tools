SECTION 1: WHAT IS PERIMETER? 🤔

Imagine you are taking a fun morning walk right along the edge of your school playground. The total distance you cover from start to finish as you go all the way around is called its PERIMETER!

💡
PERIMETER MEANS: "The total distance around the outside border of a closed shape."

1. Outside Boundary Only 🔍

We strictly measure the outer fence or border line, never the middle area!

2. Must be Closed Shapes 🛑

Perimeter only exists for fully closed figures (no open ends!).

3. Area vs Perimeter 📐

Perimeter is the length of fence; Area is the grass inside the yard!

SECTION 3: PARTS OF PERIMETER 📌

Look at this colorful rectangle. Let's name its key parts:

  • 📏 Length (L): The longer side of the shape.
  • 📐 Breadth/Width (B): The shorter side of the shape.
  • 🧱 Sides: The straight line segments forming the shape (4 sides).
  • 🚩 Boundary: The entire continuous outer path.
  • 🏃 Perimeter Path: Walking all 4 sides from start back to start!

SECTION 5: HOW TO FIND PERIMETER 🛠️

Finding perimeter is as easy as 1-2-3! Just follow these steps:

1

Look at the Outside Sides 👁️

Identify all outer straight boundary lines of the closed figure.

2

Measure Every Side 📏

Find or read the lengths given for each side (e.g., in cm, meters, inches).

3

Add All Sides Together ➕

Sum up every single side length: Perimeter = Sum of All Sides

SECTION 6: FORMULAS 📑

Smart shortcut formulas to calculate perimeter fast!

RECTANGLE

Perimeter of Rectangle

Since opposite sides are equal, add Length + Length + Breadth + Breadth:

P = 2 × (Length + Breadth)

Example: If L = 8 and B = 5, then P = 2 × (8 + 5) = 26 units.

SQUARE

Perimeter of Square

Since all 4 sides are exactly equal, multiply 1 side length by 4:

P = 4 × Side

Example: If Side = 7 cm, then P = 4 × 7 = 28 cm.

TRIANGLE

Perimeter of Triangle

A triangle has 3 sides. Simply add all three side lengths together:

P = Side 1 + Side 2 + Side 3

Example: If sides are 5 cm, 6 cm, 8 cm: P = 5 + 6 + 8 = 19 cm.

IRREGULAR SHAPE

Perimeter of Irregular Shapes

Count and add up every single outer line segment!

P = Sum of ALL Outer Sides

SECTION 7: WORKED EXAMPLES 📝

Click on any example below to reveal the step-by-step solution!

Example 1: Rectangle (8 cm × 5 cm) ▼

Question: Find the perimeter of a rectangle with Length = 8 cm and Breadth = 5 cm.

Step 1: Identify the formula: P = 2 × (Length + Breadth)

Step 2: Add Length and Breadth: 8 + 5 = 13 cm

Step 3: Multiply by 2: 2 × 13 = 26 cm

✅ Answer: Perimeter = 26 cm

Example 2: Square (Side = 7 cm) ▼

Question: Calculate perimeter of a square tile with Side = 7 cm.

Step 1: Formula for Square: P = 4 × Side

Step 2: Multiply: 4 × 7 = 28 cm

✅ Answer: Perimeter = 28 cm

Example 3: Triangle (5 cm, 6 cm, 8 cm) ▼

Question: Find the perimeter of a triangle with sides measuring 5 cm, 6 cm, and 8 cm.

Step 1: Formula: P = Side 1 + Side 2 + Side 3

Step 2: Add all 3 sides: 5 + 6 + 8 = 19 cm

✅ Answer: Perimeter = 19 cm

Example 4: Rectangle Playground (10 m × 4 m) ▼

Question: A park has length 10 m and breadth 4 m. What is its perimeter?

Solution: P = 2 × (10 + 4) = 2 × 14 = 28 m

✅ Answer: Perimeter = 28 m

Example 5: Garden Square (Side = 15 m) ▼

Question: Find the length of fence needed for a square garden with side 15 m.

Solution: P = 4 × 15 = 60 m

✅ Answer: Perimeter = 60 m

Never mix these two up again! Here is how they compare:

What it measures Inside surface / region space Outside boundary / edge distance
Units Square units (cm², m²) Standard linear units (cm, m)
Real-life Example 1 Floor carpet / tiles needed Wooden skirting border around floor
Real-life Example 2 Painting a bedroom wall Ribbon along the edges of a picture
Real-life Example 3 Lawn grass coverage Fencing wire around the garden

SECTION 9: UNITS OF PERIMETER 📏

We measure perimeter using linear metric units depending on the size of the object:

1. Millimeter (mm) 🐜

Used for tiny objects: Stamps, coins, eraser borders, small bug paths.

2. Centimeter (cm) ✏️

Used for medium objects: Notebooks, pencil boxes, photos, phone screens.

3. Meter (m) 🏫

Used for large objects: Classroom boundaries, swimming pools, gardens, yards.

4. Kilometer (km) 🚗

Used for huge distances: City boundaries, marathon tracks, large farm fields.

SECTION 10: THINKING QUESTIONS 🧠💭

🐐

Brain Teaser: The Goat & The Field!

A goat is tied with a rope to a fence post and walks all the way around the outside edge of a square field. Does the rope track measure Area or Perimeter?

Answer: PERIMETER! Because the goat is walking strictly along the outside boundary of the field.

10 Competency-Based Thinking Challenges:

Q1: If you double all side lengths of a square, what happens to its perimeter?

👉 It doubles as well!

Q2: Can two shapes have different areas but the exact same perimeter?

👉 Yes! (e.g., 4 × 4 square vs 6 × 2 rectangle both have P = 16).

Q3: A wire of length 20 cm is bent to make a square. What is each side length?

👉 20 ÷ 4 = 5 cm.

Q4: Do we count internal walls when calculating house perimeter?

👉 No! Only the outer exterior walls count.

Q5: An equilateral triangle has P = 27 cm. How long is one side?

👉 27 ÷ 3 = 9 cm.

Q6: Why do we measure perimeter in cm instead of cm²?

👉 Because perimeter measures 1D length, not 2D surface area.

Q7: A rectangle has P = 30 cm and Length = 10 cm. What is Breadth?

👉 30 ÷ 2 = 15, Breadth = 15 - 10 = 5 cm.

Q8: A runner completes 2 laps around a 50 m × 30 m field. Total distance?

👉 1 Lap = 2 × (50 + 30) = 160 m. 2 Laps = 320 m.

Q9: What shape gives the largest area for a fixed perimeter?

👉 A Circle!

Q10: If a shape is open on one side, can we find its perimeter?

👉 No, perimeter requires a fully closed figure.

SECTION 11: 15 SOLVED WORD PROBLEMS 📖

1. 🌳 Garden Fence: A square garden has a side length of 12 m. How many meters of wire fence are required to enclose it?

Sol: P = 4 × 12 = 48 meters.

2. 🏫 School Playground: A rectangular playground is 25 m long and 15 m wide. A student runs around it once. How far did they run?

Sol: P = 2 × (25 + 15) = 2 × 40 = 80 meters.

3. 📚 Book Lace: Tina wants to put a golden lace along the border of her math book (20 cm × 15 cm). How much lace is needed?

Sol: P = 2 × (20 + 15) = 70 cm.

4. 🪟 Window Frame: A square window frame has a side of 90 cm. What is its total outer perimeter?

Sol: P = 4 × 90 = 360 cm (or 3.6 meters).

5. 🖼️ Picture Frame: A triangular photo frame has sides 12 cm, 15 cm, and 18 cm. Find its perimeter.

Sol: P = 12 + 15 + 18 = 45 cm.

6. 🏊 Swimming Pool: A pool measures 30 m in length and 12 m in width. Find the total tile border length.

Sol: P = 2 × (30 + 12) = 84 m.

7. 🎁 Ribbon Box: Sam wraps ribbon around a gift box top of dimensions 18 cm × 10 cm. How much ribbon is used?

Sol: P = 2 × (18 + 10) = 56 cm.

8. 🛣️ Park Path: A triangular park has sides 100 m, 150 m, and 120 m. Find the length of the walking track around it.

Sol: P = 100 + 150 + 120 = 370 m.

9. 🏫 Classroom Display: A bulletin board is 4 m long and 2 m high. How much wooden border tape is needed?

Sol: P = 2 × (4 + 2) = 12 meters.

10. 🧱 Skirting Board: A square room has a side of 6 m. How long is the floor border skirting needed (ignoring the door)?

Sol: P = 4 × 6 = 24 meters.

11. ⚽ Football Pitch: A football field is 100 m long and 60 m wide. Calculate its total boundary perimeter.

Sol: P = 2 × (100 + 60) = 320 meters.

12. 🏷️ Name Tag: A rectangular name badge is 8 cm long and 5 cm wide. Find its border length.

Sol: P = 2 × (8 + 5) = 26 cm.

13. 🚜 Farmer's Field: A farmer has a triangular plot with sides 45 m, 55 m, and 60 m. Find total fencing needed.

Sol: P = 45 + 55 + 60 = 160 meters.

14. 📱 Tablet Screen: A tablet screen measures 24 cm by 18 cm. Find the perimeter of the screen protector.

Sol: P = 2 × (24 + 18) = 84 cm.

15. 📐 Pentagon Sandbox: A regular 5-sided sandbox has each side measuring 3 meters. Find its perimeter.

Sol: P = 5 × 3 = 15 meters.

SECTION 12: COMMON MISTAKES ❌➡️✅

❌ Mistake 1: Measuring Inside Space

Wrong: Counting interior squares or area tiles.
✅ Correct: Only trace and measure the OUTSIDE boundary lines!

❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting Side Lengths

Wrong: Adding L + B only once for a rectangle (8 + 5 = 13).
✅ Correct: Add all 4 sides! 2 × (8 + 5) = 26.

❌ Mistake 3: Wrong Units

Wrong: Writing perimeter as 26 cm².
✅ Correct: Always use linear units like cm, m, mm.

❌ Mistake 4: Multiplying Instead of Adding

Wrong: Multiplying L × B to find perimeter.
✅ Correct: Multiply is for AREA; Perimeter is SUM of sides!

SECTIONS 19 & 20: KEYWORDS & GLOSSARY 📚

🔑 Key Terms

  • 🔹 Perimeter: Total continuous outer distance.
  • 🔹 Boundary: The line enclosing a 2D shape.
  • 🔹 Closed Shape: A figure with no breaks or open ends.
  • 🔹 Dimensions: Measurements like Length and Breadth.

📖 Glossary

  • 📖 Length: The longer side measurement.
  • 📖 Breadth: The shorter side measurement.
  • 📖 Linear Units: 1D units like mm, cm, m, km.
