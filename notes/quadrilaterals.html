QUADRILATERALS

Shapes with 4 Sides, 4 Corners and 4 Angles!
WHAT IS A QUADRILATERAL?

A quadrilateral is a closed 2D (flat) shape that has:

  • ✓ 4 sides
  • ✓ 4 corners (vertices)
  • ✓ 4 angles
Quad = Four
Quadrilateral = Shape with 4 sides
PARTS OF A QUADRILATERAL
➔ Sides
AB, BC, CD, DA
➔ Vertices (Corners)
A, B, C, D
➔ Angles
∠A, ∠B, ∠C, ∠D
--➔ Diagonals
AC and BD
(Join opposite corners)

TYPES OF QUADRILATERALS
1. SQUARE
PROPERTIES
  • ★ 4 equal sides
  • ★ 4 right angles (90°)
  • ★ Opposite sides are parallel
EXAMPLES
Chessboard square
Floor tile
2. RECTANGLE
PROPERTIES
  • ★ Opposite sides are equal
  • ★ 4 right angles (90°)
  • ★ Opposite sides are parallel
EXAMPLES
Door
Book
Mobile phone
Whiteboard
3. PARALLELOGRAM
PROPERTIES
  • ★ Opposite sides are equal
  • ★ Opposite sides are parallel
  • ★ Opposite angles are equal
EXAMPLES
Slanted window
Decorative tiles
4. RHOMBUS
PROPERTIES
  • ★ All 4 sides are equal
  • ★ Opposite angles are equal
  • ★ Angles are not always 90°
EXAMPLES
Diamond symbol
Kite pattern
5. TRAPEZIUM
(TRAPEZOID)
PROPERTIES
  • ★ Only one pair of opposite sides is parallel
EXAMPLES
Bridge support
Lampshade
6. KITE
PROPERTIES
  • ★ Two pairs of equal adjacent sides
  • ★ One pair of opposite angles are equal
EXAMPLES
Flying kite
Diamond shaped decoration

REMEMBER THESE FACTS
  • ★ Every square is a rectangle.
  • ★ Every square is a rhombus.
  • ★ Not every rectangle is a square.
  • ★ Not every rhombus is a square.
  • ★ Every quadrilateral has 4 sides.
  • ★ The sum of all angles of a quadrilateral is 360°.
All quadrilaterals are closed shapes.
KEY WORDS
  • Quadrilateral – A closed shape with four sides.
  • Side – A straight line forming the shape.
  • Vertex – A corner where two sides meet.
  • Angle – The space formed by two sides meeting.
  • Diagonal – A line joining opposite corners.
  • Parallel Lines – Lines that never meet.
  • Equal Sides – Sides of the same length.
