SYMMETRY

Symmetry is when one half of a shape is the same as the other half.
It is like a perfect mirror image! 🪞⭐
WHAT IS SYMMETRY?
  • When a shape or object can be divided into two equal parts that are mirror images of each other, it is symmetrical.
  • The dividing segment or fold line is called the line of symmetry.
  • Not all shapes have symmetry—some are completely asymmetrical!
Look around you! Symmetry is found everywhere in nature and art!

QUICK FACTS
  • A symmetrical shape must contain at least one clean line of symmetry.
  • Shapes can contain multiple distinct axes lines (e.g. circles contain infinite axes).
  • The line of symmetry always bisects a target shape into matching identical halves.
  • Verification tests include structural folding mappings or clear mirror line checks.
If a mirror reflection perfectly completes the original object, it is symmetrical!

HOW TO CHECK SYMMETRY?
✏️
1. Draw a Test Line Draw a line down the middle of the shape. If both sides are exactly identical mirror components, it possesses symmetry.
🪞
2. Use a Mirror Place a straight hand mirror directly along your proposed centerline split. If the mirror reflection cleanly matches the hidden side, your line is correct.

THINK AND ANSWER
KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER
  • Symmetry means identical halves that match up perfectly when folded.
  • The dividing bisector line is called the line of symmetry.
  • A shape can contain 0, 1, 2, or many lines of symmetry.
  • We check for symmetry easily by folding or using standard mirror lines.
  • Symmetry creates balance, composition, and beauty in design and nature!
