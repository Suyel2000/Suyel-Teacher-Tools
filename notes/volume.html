💡 Section 1: What is Volume?

Imagine you have an empty toy box. How many toys can fit inside?

That inside space is called Volume!

Volume means: "The amount of 3D space inside an object."

  • 📦 Volume measures inside space.
  • 🧃 It tells how much something can hold.
  • 🧊 We use unit cubes to measure it.
  • 📐 Volume is different from Area (flat surface) and Perimeter (outline)!

📐 Section 6 & 7: Formulas for Volume

📦 Cuboid Formula

Volume = Length × Breadth × Height

V = L × B × H

🧊 Cube Formula

Volume = Side × Side × Side

V = S³

⚠️ Note: Volume is ALWAYS written in cubic units (like cm³, m³, or cubic units)!

📝 Section 8: Worked Examples

Example 1: Find Volume of a Cuboid

Length (L) = 5 cm, Breadth (B) = 3 cm, Height (H) = 2 cm

Solution:

Volume = L × B × H

Volume = 5 × 3 × 2

Volume = 30 cm³

Example 2: Find Volume of a Cube

Side (S) = 4 cm

Solution:

Volume = Side × Side × Side

Volume = 4 × 4 × 4

Volume = 64 cm³

🧪 Section 11: Units of Volume

Solid Units:

  • 🔹 Cubic Centimetre (cm³): Used for small objects like dice, juice boxes.
  • 🔹 Cubic Metre (m³): Used for large spaces like rooms, swimming pools.

Liquid Units (Capacity):

  • 🥛 Millilitre (mL): Small liquid amounts (medicine, spoonfuls).
  • 🪣 Litre (L): Large liquid amounts (water bottles, buckets).

🧠 Section 12 & 13: Fun Word Problems

Q1: A fish tank has a length of 10 cm, breadth of 4 cm, and height of 5 cm. What is its volume?

Volume = L × B × H = 10 × 4 × 5 = 200 cm³!

Q2: An ice cube has a side length of 3 cm. How much space does it occupy?

Volume = Side³ = 3 × 3 × 3 = 27 cm³!

⚠️ Section 14 & 15: Watch Out & Quick Tips!

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Multiplying only two sides (that gives Area!).
  • Forgetting to add height.
  • Writing cm² instead of cm³.

✅ Quick Tips

  • Always count all 3 dimensions (L, B, H).
  • Remember: Cubes fill up 3D space!
  • Write "cm³" or "cubic units" in answers.
